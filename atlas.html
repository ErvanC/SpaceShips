" ATLAS V "

Overview

Atlas V is an expendable launch system and the fifth major version in the Atlas launch vehicle family. It was originally designed by Lockheed Martin, now being operated by United Launch Alliance (ULA), a joint venture between Lockheed Martin and Boeing.

• Diameter: 3.81 m

• Mass: 334,500 kg

• Capacity: 120 People

• Payload to Leo: 8,900 kg

• Payload to GTO: 4,850 kg

• Payload to Mars: 2,020 kg kg

Engine

The Atlas V rocket uses a powerful engine called the RD-180, which provides the main propulsion for the rocket

• Number of Engines: Single engine (RD-180)

• Thrust at sea level: 3,827 kN

• Thrust in vacuum: 4,152 kN
